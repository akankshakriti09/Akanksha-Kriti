Hathi Kua

Hathi Kua is an ancient well in which elephants of Kings used to drink waters.It was built by Shershah Suri around 500 yrs ago. This well is situated in Sasaram near Shershah Suri Tomb. There was a door on the upper water level of the well in the elephant well and a slope from the ground reached the door of the well. The elephants used to go through that shield path from the ground and reach the water of the well and came back after drinking water.

In recent time water of Hathi kua is used in irrigation.The local people called this well "Hathiya Enar".

Pic 1: Door in the well of eight initiatives

In Maurya dynasty, there is much importance of Elephants. There were nine thousand elephants on thirty thousand horses in Maurya dynasty whereas in Suri dynasty, there were only three hundred elephants on 1.5 million horses . Estimates are supposed to be the inventor of the elephant well may have been Maurya. Texture of wells gives us remembrance of the dharma wheel, or dharmachakra which is one of the oldest symbols of Buddhism. There are eight spokes in the Dharmachakra and elephant well has eight initiatives. In the first picture there is a door in the well of eight initiatives. The second picture is of the sloping path that reaches the doors of the well. The third picture is made in the middle of the road, in which there are eight initiatives.
