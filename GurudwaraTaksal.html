Gurudwara Taksal Sangat

Gurudwara Taksal Sangat is a very significant religious center in Sasaram, Rohtas district of Bihar State, which is popular because of its committed activities towards Sikh education and practice.This Gurudwara, like most of the Gurudwaras in Sasaram and in few other regions of Bihar State, was initiated by the ninth Guru of Sikhism Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji and now ably managed by the exclusive committee of Agrahais Sikhs.'

Pic 1: Gurudwara Taksal Sangat

In this Gurudwara, relics of a steel arrow said to be that of Guru Tegh Bahadur and a rare picture of Guru Gobind Singh Ji and Mata JioJee are available for the visitors to see.

History

Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji used to travel through Bihar area during his Eastern tours leading up to Assam, where once he entered the place is now where the Gurudwara is situated. This place was then a tobacco growing field, Guruji who was riding a horse got stuck up here. Seeing the location, Guruji expressed his wish to construct a Gurudwara here naming it Taksal Sangat. The owners of the land Bhai Achal Ji and Mata Jio Ji, happily donated their land for this noble purpose and the Gurudwara was raised. In this Gurdwara, there is a steel arrow said to be of Guru Tegh Bahadur along with a rare picture of Guru Gobind Singh Ji and of Mata Jio Jee.

Pic 2: Guru Granth Sahib

A new Gurudwara building has been constructed in recent times here with the initiative of Baba Raghuvir Singh, the Nirmala Sant from Kankhal Haridwar.

Celebrations

Sant Raghbir Singh Ji, a Nirmala Sant from Kankhal Haridwar has initiated regular Gurudwara activities such as daily 'Paath' and 'Simran' rendered particularly by females in morning. Also regular Gurmat classes are conducted where Sikh children, and children of other religions too, to learn and practice Gurmukhi, Kirtan, Gurbani etc.
