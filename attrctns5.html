Akbarpur is one beautiful place where the lovers of medieval history will be drawn to. It is located at the foot of the famous Kaimur hills nearly 5 km away from Rohtasgarh. Akbarpur has been named in the memory of the great Mughal ruler, Akbar. This tourist spot is situated very near the Rohtas headquarters. .It contains the tomb of Malik Wishal Khan, the daroga of Rohtas Garh during the reign of emperor Shahjahan. The inscription in Persian on the tomb indicates that Akbarpur was a pergora during the Mughal period. The attraction you are not to miss here is the tomb of Malik Wishal Khan who was the daroga of Rohtasgarh when Shahjahan was the emperor.

Indrapuri Barrage (also known as the Sone Barrage) is across the Son River in Rohtas district. The Indrapuri Barrage at Indrapuri is 1,407 metres (4,616 ft) long and is the fourth longest barrage in the world. It was constructed by HCC ( Hindustan Construction Company), the company which constructed the 2,253 m long Farakka Barrage, the longest in the world. Construction of the Indrapuri barrage was taken up in the 1960s and it was commissioned in 1968.
