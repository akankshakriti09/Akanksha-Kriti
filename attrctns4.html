Tourist Attractions
31

Rohtasgarh Fort is located in the southern region of Dehri about 40 km away on Kaimur range and it is 1500 m above sea level. The founder of Rohtasgarh town was Rohitashva, son of the the great king Harishchandra. The Rohtasgarh Fort is one of the most ancient forts in India. This tourist spot stands as a symbol of courage, strength and supremacy of the Sone valley.

The Rohtasgarh fort itself has great places which capture the attention of tourists like Ganesha Temple, Elephant Gate, The Hanging House, Hathiya Pol, the Aina Mahal, Habsh Khan Mausoleum, Jama Masjid... Read More

Rohtasgarh Fort
32

The Rohtasan Devi Temple are located almost a mile towards the northeast of the Rohtasgarh palace. The Rohtasan Devi temple is situated a mile to the North-East of the Man Singh’s Palace. The temple was constructed by King Harishchandra. Devi temple is a ruined temple and the deity inside the temple is missing. This temple is dedicated to Maa Durga. The idol of the deity is missing from here even, but the rest of the construction is still in good condition.

Rohtasan Devi Temple
33

The Chaurashan Shiv Mandir are located almost a mile towards the northeast of the Rohtasgarh palace. The Chaurashan Mandir is situated a mile to the North-East of the Man Singh's Palace. The temple is dedicated Lord Shiva. Iconoclasts probably destroyed the roof and the main mandap, which contained the sacred lingam. The idol of the deity is missing from here even, but the rest of the construction is still in good condition. Presently, there are just 84 steps are left, which pave way to the temple built by King Harishchandra. In local language, it is also called as "Chourasan Siddhi" because of its 84 steps... Read More

34

Ganesha Temple is situated to the west of Man Singh palace. The architecture of the temple is also based on Rajputana style and design is based on the temples constructed in Jodhpur and Chittorgarh. Therefore Temple look like Osian temple of Jodhpur built in the 8th century AD. Another temple is the Mira Bai temple of Chittor the 17th century AD old. The temple is dedicated Lord Ganesha.

Ganesha Temple
35

One kilometer north of Rohtas Fort is Bawan Talab known for ancient temples. Bawan Talab is a popular tourist attraction for its religious significance. Devotees visit this place to offer their prayers to the god. The pond is located just one kilometer north of Rohtas Fort. This place is known for the existence of many ancient temples. As the name conveys, there are 52 ponds surrounding the region during the good olden days. Currently, there is no trace of those ponds now.

Bawan Talab
36

Aina Mahal, which is located in the middle of the fort, was built by Raja Man Singh. The palace has four storeys having a cupola on the top. Assembly hall was built on the second floor. Third floor has women quarters and tourists can enter there through a tiny cupola. First floor has the residential quarters of Man Singh and there is a gate called Baradari which connects it to ladies rooms.

This is the palace of the chief wife of Man-Singh. The middle part of palace was resident for Man-singh. Palace is a four-storied building where, Man-Singh himself resided and was called 'Takhte Badshahi'.

Aaina Mahal

This is Diwan- E- Aam of Rohtasgarh Fort, Bihar situated on the Kaimur hills. It was a place dedicated to common people where the king used to hear and decide the grievances of his people.

Diwan-E-Aam
38

This is Diwan- E- Khas of Rohtasgarh Fort, Bihar situated on the Kaimur hills. An assembly hall , probably the Diwan-e-Khas or the hall or private audience is a little towards the west of Baradari or the hall of public audience. The hall is decorated with etchings of flowers and leaves, and lies on similarly decorated pillars.

Diwan-E-Khas
39

Outside the Man Singh’s palace grounds has the buildings of Jama Masjid. This is a Habsh Khan’s Mausoleum and the Makbara of Shufi Sultan. This Masjid are beautiful structures which were made through combination of rajputana style and beautiful stucco style. The architecture of the buildings is of Rajputana style as there are cupolas on the pillars. The architecture of the buildings is of Rajputana style as there are domes on the pillars.

Jama Masjid & Habsh Khan Mausoleum
40

Further towards west of Ganesha temple, some construction must have taken place although there is no written evidence of what it was. The locals call it the Hanging House, as the fall from here is a straight 1500 ft down with no obstacles on the way. Locals have a story to tell about this place that this spot is the mouth of a cave, where a Muslim fakir (mendicant) is buried. It is said that he was thrown from here into the valley three times. In spite of being bound hand and foot, the fakir escaped unhurt each time. Ultimately he was buried here alive in the cave.
