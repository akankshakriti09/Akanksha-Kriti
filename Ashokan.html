The Ashokan Inscription and rock-cut cave is located on the Chandan Shaheed Hill near the Chand Tan Pir. These are ancient rock cut caves locally called as Chirag Dan. Their most significant feature is an inscription of 8 lines written on its walls in Archaic Brahmi Characters.

In the rock shelter or the cave there is another stone, attached obliquely with the engraved rock on which a major or grave of some Kajaliya Baba is now under worship and is under the possession of muslim community who have constructed a small wall in front portion of the rock shelter for fixing an iron gate.The rock shelter is very small having a height of only three feet and its interior space can accommodate hardly two persons inside the rock shelter.It seems that this rock shelter, having Asokan Minor Rock edict is under possession of Muslim community for quite longer period and they are applying lime coat/white washing regularly, with the result that the inscription is getting invisible.The text of this inscription was published by A. Cunningham, Buhler, Senat and Hultzsch and others scholars.

Emperor Ashoka's small inscription in crisis

Archaeological view of the Emperor Ashoka's small inscription on the most important Kaimur hill is locked in the lock and its existence is in crisis. In 10 years, the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) called on the inscription to take possession of the inscription several times but the administration did not pay attention. As a result, it has been imprisoned in the small room of Kaimur hill since the National Heritage Year.

In the east of 256 BC, small inscriptions of Emperor Ashoka were established at eight places in the country. The only inscription in Bihar was established near Chandan Shaheed Mazar of Kaimur hill of Sasaram. This inscription provides international importance to Sasaram.

"Allen f Antleen Jambudipasis" is the first line of this inscription. This line, written in the Brahmi script, means "People of all faiths in Jambu Island, India, be tolerant." Inscription inscriptions in the iron door has been leased to the lime several times. It has its existence in crisis.
