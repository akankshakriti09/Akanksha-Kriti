Hathi Kua is an ancient well in which elephants of Kings used to drink waters.It was built by Shershah Suri around 500 yrs ago. This well is situated in Sasaram near Shershah Suri Tomb. There was a door on the upper water level of the well in the elephant well and a slope from the ground reached the door of the well. The elephants used to go through that shield path from the ground and reach the water of the well and came back after drinking water.

In recent time water of Hathi kua is used in irrigation.The local people called this well "Hathiya Enar"...Read More

While Alawal Khan was incharge of the construction of Sher Shah's tomb he pilfered better materials for his own tomb and was therefore put to disgrace by Sher Shah. The reason behind the name of the waterfall is dense fog that covers around the area almost all time of the year. The fog looks like smoke from far and that is why it has been named as the Dhua Kund where Dhua means Smoke.The significance of the waterfall is immense and beyond just a tourist destination.
