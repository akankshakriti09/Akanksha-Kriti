Gurudwara Sri Chacha Phaggu Mal
Pic 1: Gurudwara Sri Chacha Phaggu Mal

The Gurdwara Chacha Phaggu Mal marking the site of the old masand's house where the Guru stayed. This is the principal Gurdwara of the town housed in a spacious high-ceilinged hall with a gallery at mid-height. Three copies of Guru Granth Sahib are seated side by side under a domed rectangular pavilion of white marble.

Pic 2: Three copies of Guru Granth Sahib

Gurudwara Sri Chacha Phagu Mal Sahib is situated in Sasaram Town, Dist Rohtas Bihar. Bhai Phaggu Mall (also known as Chacha Phaggu Mall) - a Gurmukh, Masand and Devout of Gurughar, was a follower of Guru Amar Das. To spread the message of Sikhism Guru Amar Das as a signature sent Manji's (Bed's) to different direction of the world from Goindwal Sahib. Out of them one Manji was sent to Sasaram, Bihar through Bhai Phaggu Mal ji. Once Phaggu mal ji reached Bihar, he stayed there for rest of his life and became the care taker of this location which was adopted to preach Sikhism.

There is a significant memory associated with Sikh history in Sasaram. In the jani market of the city is the historical Gurudwara Chacha Faggulal , where 9th Guru Teghbahadur Ji of the Sikhs lived for 21 days and got the supporters happy with their blessings. But no plaque was given to the Sasaram railway station giving information about this historic gurdwara. The distance of this Gurudwara to the railway station is just two kilometers. However the way to go is through narrow streets. The devotees can reach the Jani Market through the Dharamshala Road of the city to Gola and then the Navaratna market or even from post office chowk through Lashkari Ganj to Jani Market.

Darshini Darwaja & Chacha Phaggumal Khadau and Naam Simarni Mala

This 45- inch high , 35- inch wide door is known by the name of Darshini Darwaja, through which Navam Patshahi ji entered in Chacha Phaggumal's cottage. This door can be seen in the original form even today. Nasim Patshahi can be seen in the gurudwara of Arshad Sahib , Chacha Phaggumal Khadau and Naam Simarni Mala, which were presented to the accompanist. There are 76 beads in this garland .

During his stay, he used to collect all the offerings (Chadaava) that Devotes used to donate and submit it to the Guru Sahib on his regular visits to them. He continued this practice with 6 different Guru Sahib's. Bhai Phaggu Mal Ji was the second person in the sikh history (After Baba Budha Ji Sahib) who had met and took blessing of Six Guru Sahib's (Guru Amar Das to Guru Tegh Bahadur). However, once Bhai Phagu Mal became old he couldn't travel and prayed for Guru Ji to visit him one last time before he re-joined with God.

In his old age, Bhai Phaggu Mal Ji used to meditate and pray a lot to see Guru Sahib live as he felt that he is too old and cannot travel now. On this when Sri Tegh Bahadur ji went on tour in the east direction he heard Bhai Phagu Mal's prayers and decided to see him and went to his house personally. When Guru Sahib reached his home, Bhai Phaggu Mal Ji was lying on bed. Someone told him that Guru Sahib had come. Bhai Sahib said if there is Guru Sahib then he don't need my permission. A small door opened (That door is still preserved in Gurudwara Sahib) and Guru Sahib entered the room where Bhai Sahib was lying. Guru Sahib addressed Bhai Phaggu Mal Ji as "Chacha Phaggu Mal Ji". Phaggu Mall himself received Guru Sahib, his family and congregation with charm and did selfless service, during thier stay at Sasaram. Bhai Phaggu Mal was overjoyed and took personal care of Guru Ji with his family and the Sangat during the Guru's stay at Sasaram.

Plum tree planted by Guruji

More than 350 years old plum tree planted by Guruji is present in the Gurudwara complex. This tree is still green. It gives fruit throughout the year. There may be a visit to that posture on which Guru ji used to take bath daily during his stay at Sasaram.

Ninth Sikh Guru Tez Bahadur stays 21 days in Sasaram

It is said that the sum of nine fund houses resides in the Suman of Ninth Deodhahi Guru Teghbahadur. The people of Sasaram were blessed, who received the blessings of the sheet of Ninth Patshahi of Hind for many days. In the year 1666 , Guru Teghbahadur came out on his sadness (journey) with the east of the Lashkar. During this visit, he was on a visit to Kashi from Patiala by giving a message of 'japa' and 'wand chhicha'. During this he stayed for 21 days in Sasaram . Here he stayed in the cottage of Nanaka Shahi Chacha Faggulal. He was accompanied by his mother Nanaki , his Dharmapati mother Gujari and wife Bhrata Baba Kripal Chand.

Apart from making this historic city holy, the Ninth Patshahi also did many compositions while staying there. Here, the chant Jajayvanti Mahla , the voice of Dhoom , Ram Sumari Ram Sumuri is your keji , Maaya is called Sarani Tiagu Prabhu's Sarani , Jagat Sukh Manu Mithiya Jhooto Sabasuju. The Guruvani was of Bakshish. The discussion is glorious on the 1352 pages of Guru Granth Sahib .

Confirmation of these historical facts is also in the cult certified texts Suraj Prakash and Tawarik Guru Khalsa. This has been done in Gurunak Dev Dev University Amritsar and Punjabi University Patiala.

Chacha Phaggumal was a non-Sikh saint who came to Bihar to campaign for the cult from Phagwara of Punjab. After that, they remained here. When Nava Guru Teghbahadur came to Sasaram, Chacha Phaggumal felicitated him. This historic gurus of Sasaram have many memories related to the ninth polity.

Construction of New Darbar

The new Darbar Sahib of gurudwara chacha Phaggumal has been constructed in the year 2012. In its creation, Jathedar Sant , Baba Harivansh Singh Ji's role is played by Gurdwara Bangla Sahib. Efforts are on to make the construction work of anchor hall and passenger resident right now. In the gurudwara, Aradas is in the form of regular Satsanga, Shri Akal Takht Sahib.
